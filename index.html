Wheels for Feels

Basic car information

  1. Engine: The heart of the car, responsible for converting fuel into mechanical energy to move the vehicle.
  2. Transmission: Connects the engine to the wheels, allowing the car to change gears and control speed.
  3. Brakes: A critical safety system that allows the car to slow down or stop by applying friction to the wheels.
  4. Suspension: Provides a smooth ride by absorbing shocks from the road and maintaining tire contact with the ground.
  5. Steering: Allows the driver to control the direction of the car by turning the wheels.
  6. Mileage: Refers to the distance a car can travel on a specific amount of fuel, usually measured in miles per gallon (MPG) or kilometers per liter (km/L). It's a key factor in determining fuel efficiency.
  7. RPM (Revolutions Per Minute): Measures how many times the engine's crankshaft makes a full rotation every minute. It indicates how hard the engine is working; higher RPMs generally mean more power but can lead to greater fuel consumption.
